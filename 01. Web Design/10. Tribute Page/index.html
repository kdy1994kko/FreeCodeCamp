Muhammad Ali

One Of "The Greatest" Boxers Of All Time

"The Greatest" time line of Muhammad Ali's life:

  • 1942 - January 17th. . Born as Cassius Marcellus Clay Jr. in Louisville, Kentucky
  • 1954 - As the story goes, one night in 1954, 12-year-old Cassius Clay was attending an event in a building that housed the Columbia Gym in his Louisville, Kentucky, hometown. When he came outside, he discovered his new red Schwinn bicycle was missing. Clay was first directed toward boxing by Louisville police officer and boxing coach Joe E. Martin,[40] who encountered the 12-year-old fuming over a thief's having taken his bicycle. He told the officer he was going to "whup" the thief. The officer told Clay he had better learn how to box first. Clay made his amateur boxing debut. Clay's amateur record was 100 wins with 5 losses.
  • 1959 - After more years of climbing up the ranks, Clay won the Golden Gloves Tournament of Champion, and the National AAU light-heavyweight championship.
  • 1960 - September 5th. .Cassius Clay, defeats Poland's Zbigniew Pietrzykowski to win the gold medal in the light heavyweight division at the 1960 Rome Olympics. October 29. . Clay made his professional debut.
  • 1964 - February 25th. . “Float like a butterfly, sting like a bee. The hands can’t hit what the eyes can’t see.” At 20 wins with 0 losses, Cassius Clay defeats Sonny Liston to win the WBA, WBC, NYSAC, and The Ring heavyweight titles. "I must be The Greatest. I shook up the world!" March 6th. . He changes his name to Muhammad Ali.
  • 1971 - March 8th. . After 31 wins with 0 losses, Ali suffers his first defeat to Joe Frazier to lose the WBA, WBC, and The Ring heavyweight titles.
  • 1974 - January 28th. . After 44 wins with 2 losses, Ali defeats Joe Frazier to retaine his NABF heavyweight title. October 30th. . ( later that year ) Ali wins the WBA, WBC, and The Ring heavyweight titles by defeating George Foreman during The Rumble In The Jungle. While the Congolese audience cheered and shouted "Ali boma ye !!" ("Ali Kill Him !!")
  • 1981 - Ali was truely one of "The Greatest." 37 knockouts later, he retires with a career record of 56 wins, 5 losses.
  • 1984 - After checking himself into Columbia Presbyterian Hospital, Ali was given a devastating diagnosis: Parkinson's disease.
  • 2016 - June 3rd. . Muhammad Ali dies at the age of 74 in Scottsdale, Arizona.

"I'm blessed and thankful to God that I understand he's trying me. This is a trial from God. He gave me this illness to remind me that I am not number one; He is."

-- Muhammad Ali reflecting on having Parkinson's disease
